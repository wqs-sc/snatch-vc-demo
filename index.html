User App • Sponsored Hunt Flow

This demo showcases how a player discovers, approaches, and snatches the sponsored campaign launched in the Campaign Studio. Note the live sync: launching the Nike Sneaker hunt triggers it on the mobile device!

Core Player Journey

  • 1. Discover: The player sees a holographic Nike sneaker floating in their camera view, pinned to a specific geofenced location in Soho.
  • 2. Approach: Tapping the item opens the proximity tracker. The distance meter shows how many meters away the prize is. The player must walk closer.
  • 3. Snatch: Once within range (< 25m), the glowing "SNATCH IT" trigger becomes active. Clicking it locks the reward.
  • 4. Wallet: The reward lands in the wallet with its corresponding redemption barcode/code, driving the user directly in-store or online.

VC Pitch Angle

Traditional location check-ins are boring. Snatch makes the brand drop feel like high-fidelity video gameplay (like finding rare chest loot in RPGs) while maintaining zero friction.

Quick Hunt • Social Loop (C2C)

The social engine of Snatch. Quick Hunts are lightweight, user-generated campaigns. Users hide digital boxes or real-world prizes for their friends, offices, campuses, or communities, driving organic viral growth.

Quick Hunt Flow Stages

1. Configure Details

User specifies name ("Campus Coffee Hunt"), prize ("Starbucks for the winner"), visibility parameters, and duration limit.

2. Geotag & Drop

User switches on camera, places the holographic Starbucks cup on the floor, and writes a location hint ("Near the lounge").

3. Invite & Rally

App generates a unique QR Code and join code ("CAMPUS25"). Friends scanning the code join the live crew lobby instantly.

4. The Player Hunt

Invited players follow the clue, track their distance indicator on the camera screen, scan the area, and snatch the prize.

C2C Viral Wedge

Sponsored brand drops fund the ecosystem, but Quick Hunts drive daily organic traffic. One user creating a hunt invites 5-10 friends, turning players into active recruiters.

1 The Phone Simulator has been set to the Quick Hunt Creator.
2 Click the buttons on the simulator screen to go through the configuration, placement, and join lobby.

Snatch Mode • Core Gamification

The always-on retention layer. When there are no sponsored campaigns active, Snatch Mode allows players to level up, upgrade radar range/abilities, and compete for regional dominance on the SoHo leaderboard.

Progression Mechanics

  • XP & Levels: Earn experience points with every successful hunt. Leveling up unlocks premium drops and advanced tools.
  • Scan Radius Upgrades: Use ability points earned to increase radar pulse range, revealing hidden rewards further away.
  • Status Badges: Earning badges like "Fast Finder" or "Local Legend" validates user activity and unlocks exclusive vendor collaborations.

Ecosystem Retention

Snatch Mode turns physical walking routes into a reward gathering grid. Users build daily routine habits around the app, maintaining high retention rates that keep the network liquid.

Local Ambassadors • Distribution Scaling

How Snatch scales distribution without massive advertising costs. The top 1% of players in any district are recruited as "Snatch Ambassadors." They are equipped with campaign builder tools to seed games, create local drops, and recruit players.
